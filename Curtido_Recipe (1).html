    Curtido Recipe

    Curitdo is a pickled slaw that is primarily made with cabbage, carrots, and green chili peppers and is also one of El Salvador's national foods. Curtido is treated as a topping or a side, so versatile that you can pair it with any dish. This is my grandmother's recipe, she perfected a spicy and savory curtido which I absolutely love.

    Ingredients:

    • One cabbage

    • One carrot

    • One green serrano pepper

    • 1 cup water

    • 1/3 cup distilled white vinegar

    • 1 tbsp salt

    • Oregano

    Kitchenware:

    • Cutting board

    • Cutting knife

    • Bowl with lid

    • Pot

    Prep:

    Cabbage

    1. Place your cabbage on your cutting board and cut it in half, then cut both halves in half. You should have four wedges of cabbage. Leave only one wedge on the board, seal the other wedges however you want, and place it in your fridge.

    2. Cut the stem off that is at one end of the wedge.

    3. Place your knife perpendicular to the cabbage wedge and proceed to cut the cabbage into thick shreds

    4. From the board, place the shredded cabbage into your bowl.

    Carrots

    1. Place a carrot on your cutting board and hold it by its end.

    2. Cut the carrot into slices.

    3. With those slices, cut them down into more slices.

    4. Place the sliced carrots in the bowl.

    Green serrano pepper

    1. Place your pepper on the cutting board and hold it by the end of the stem head.

    2. Use your knife to cut the pepper into thin slices.

    3. Cut until you reach close to the stem head and toss the head.

    4. Place the sliced pepper into the bowl.

    Set the bowl with all the vegetables aside, we'll move on to the pickling process.

    Pickling:

    1. Grab your pot and place it over your stove.

    2. Set the stove to a medium heat.

    3. Pour in the water, salt, and vinegar.

    4. Wait until the mixture is simmering, tiny bubbles should start forming and there will be steam.

    Grab your bowl with the vegetables and place it next to the stove.

    1. From the pot pour in the liquid mixture into the bowl.

    2. Grab a fork or whatever utensil you have on hand and mix the vegetables around so that the pickling mixture has reached all the vegetables.

    3. Cover the bowl with a lid and set it aside for more than 8 hours.

    Results: After leaving the curtido out for more than 8 hours it is ready to serve, the taste should be savory, spicy, and almost tangy. * hours is the minimum but it is best served after 24 hours of sitting.
